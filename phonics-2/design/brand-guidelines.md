# Phonics.org Brand Guidelines (Draft v1)

## 1) Brand Direction
Phonics.org should feel like a premium educational resource: evidence-based, refined, and trustworthy.

- Personality: expert, clear, encouraging, sophisticated (never childish or gimmicky).
- Positioning: authoritative educational hub that supports families and educators with depth and care, creating natural pathways to Reading.com.
- Visual stance: editorial excellence meets modern utility—think high-quality magazine meets practical learning resource (not app-like, not classroom-clipart, not generic stock imagery).

## 2) Creative Direction (inspired by Reading.com, but distinct)
Shared cues with Reading.com:
- warm, optimistic education tone
- clear, confident conversion CTAs
- accessible, parent-friendly language
- professional polish and quality feel

Deliberate differences for Phonics.org:
- **More editorial and research-forward**: content-rich layouts with clear visual hierarchy
- **Deeper information architecture**: robust wayfinding, clear pathways, category-based navigation
- **Premium photography**: authentic learning moments, diverse families, real classroom environments
- **Clean, generous whitespace**: let content breathe, avoid visual clutter
- **Refined section framing**: use subtle backgrounds, photography, and color accents (no heavy gradients)
- **Sophisticated silo color system**: strategic use of category colors for wayfinding

## 3) Logo Direction
Use a wordmark + symbol lockup:
- Symbol concept: open book + directional path (signals learning progression)
- Wordmark: `Phonics.org` in Fraunces (serif) for authority
- Clear space: at least the x-height of the wordmark
- Minimum width: 132px digital
- **Treatment**: Clean, flat colors only—no gradients, no effects. Premium simplicity.

## 5) Typography
Type system:
- All text: `Nunito` (friendly, modern, highly readable sans-serif)
- Display/headline weight: 800-900 (bold, authoritative)
- Body/UI weight: 400-600 (clean, comfortable)

Scale:
- H1: 52/58 desktop, 38/44 mobile
- H2: 36/42 desktop, 30/36 mobile
- H3: 24/30
- Body: 18/30
- Small/meta: 14/22

Rules:
- Keep paragraph width to ~70ch max.
- Use sentence case for headings.
- Use heavier weights (800-900) for headlines to maintain authority and presence.

## 6) Spacing + Layout
- Base unit: 8px
- Section rhythm: 72px vertical desktop, 48px mobile
- Container max width: 1200px
- Grid:
  - Desktop: 12 columns
  - Tablet: 8 columns
  - Mobile: 4 columns

## 7) Component Patterns
- Buttons:
  - Primary: filled `brand-500`, rounded 14px, bold label
  - Secondary: transparent with `ink-900` border
  - Text CTA: inline arrow treatment
- Cards:
  - Soft radius (20px)
  - Subtle border + shadow
  - Lift on hover (2-4px)
- Navigation:
  - Sticky top bar
  - Active item gets underline + color tint
- Tags/Pills:
  - Silo-color backgrounds at low tint
  - Consistent 999px rounding

## 8) Imagery + Iconography

### Photography Guidelines (Critical for Premium Feel)
**Source high-quality images from**: Unsplash, Pexels, or custom photography

**Preferred subject matter**:
- Authentic learning moments: children reading with parents/teachers in natural light
- Diverse, real families in home environments
- Classroom scenes that feel warm and engaged (not staged)
- Close-up detail shots: books, letters, hands writing, etc.
- Bright, naturally-lit environments

**Technical requirements**:
- High resolution (minimum 2000px wide for hero images)
- Natural color tones that complement our palette
- Avoid: overly saturated images, dated stock photography, posed corporate looks, blue-gradient backgrounds

**Image treatment**:
- Light duotone overlays acceptable for brand consistency
- Subtle vignettes to direct focus
- Never apply heavy filters or obvious gradients
- Maintain authentic skin tones

### Iconography
- Style: rounded geometric line icons; consistent 2px stroke weight
- Use sparingly—let photography and typography lead
- Silo category icons should be simple, recognizable symbols

## 9) Motion
- Keep motion functional and brief (180-240ms).
- Recommended:
  - staggered card reveal on load
  - gentle hover lift
  - button press scale at 0.98

## 10) What to Avoid (Premium Quality Control)
**Never use**:
- ❌ Cheap gradients (especially blue→purple, orange→pink trendy fades)
- ❌ Heavy drop shadows or excessive depth effects
- ❌ Cartoon clipart or illustrated characters
- ❌ Generic stock photography (businesspeople shaking hands, stock smiles)
- ❌ Overly playful or childish treatments
- ❌ Busy background patterns or textures
- ❌ Too many colors fighting for attention

**Instead, prioritize**:
- ✓ Flat, solid color backgrounds or subtle paper textures
- ✓ High-quality photography with real moments
- ✓ Generous whitespace
- ✓ Strategic use of one or two accent colors per section
- ✓ Clean, legible typography hierarchy
- ✓ Sophisticated restraint

## 11) Accessibility Baseline
- Target WCAG 2.1 AA contrast.
- Focus state: visible 2px outline + offset.
- Touch targets: 44px minimum.
- Never encode silo meaning by color alone; pair color + text/icon.

## 12) Content Tone
- Direct and evidence-first.
- Explain terms quickly, then link to deeper pages.
- CTA language should be helpful first, product second.
